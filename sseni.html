2023 Sato Seni
Knitting and Spinning
in Contemporary Japan

In 2023, Vincent Meyrick was awarded the George Alexander Fellowship by the International Specialised Skills Institute to further their studies around knitwear and fashion design. They interned Sato Seni, one of Japan’s largest and most established knitwear companies, based in the northern Yamagata prefecture. They trained in Japan for six months. Working closely with friend and mentor Tesshun Sato, they immersed themselves in the Japanese language and way of life. During their internship, they became familiar with the hands-on practices, programming and engineering required to produce knitwear from start to finish. They not only learnt about knitwear techniques, but about the cultural landscape textiles came from. They documented this process to better understand how to re-vitalise Australia’s own textile manufacturing industry.

The photographs below were taken mostly in a small town called Sagae in Yamagata. They are a chronology of the different stages of manufacturing knitwear and yarn: from wool preparation, wool spinning, yarn winding and twisting, and dyeing, through to different knitting processes, sewing, pattern making, cutting, steaming, product checking and packaging.

After their internship, Vincent travelled around Japan, visiting the denim mills and jean factories of Okayama, and working as a styling assistant at Vogue in Tokyo.
